Dalat Loop

A circular route through the heart of the Central Highlands — Dalat to Ta Dung Lake, across to Lak Lake, and back to Dalat. Around 550 kilometres of some of the most spectacular riding in Vietnam.

Tour Itinerary

Starting and finishing in Dalat — your guide picks you up at your hotel.

Day 1: Dalat to Ta Dung Lake

We leave Dalat and head south-west toward Ta Dung Lake — a remote reservoir in the middle of a national park, surrounded by forested hills. The route passes through coffee and pepper plantations and isolated minority villages. Overnight at Ta Dung.

Day 2: Ta Dung Lake to Lak Lake

From Ta Dung we push east toward Lak Lake, the largest natural freshwater lake in the Central Highlands. The landscape is wide open and dramatic. We overnight in a traditional M'Nong longhouse on the lakeshore — one of the most memorable nights on any tour we run.

Day 3: Lak Lake Back to Dalat

After an optional morning canoe on the lake, we loop back south and west to Dalat, completing the circle. The return route takes different roads, offering fresh scenery and more hidden stops along the way. Drop-off at your hotel in the afternoon.
