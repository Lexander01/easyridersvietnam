Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about riding the Central Highlands and coastal roads of Vietnam.

Do I need a motorcycle license?

If you ride as a pillion passenger with our professional guides, you do not need a license. If you choose the self-drive option, you must have a valid international driving permit (IDP) that explicitly covers motorcycles.

What happens to my heavy luggage or suitcases?

Our motorbikes are equipped with heavy-duty custom racks. We securely strap down your backpacks, suitcases, and bags in waterproof covers before we hit the road. Your luggage travels safely behind you for the entire trip.

I have zero motorbike experience. Can I still do the tour?

Yes. The majority of our clients ride pillion (on the back) with our experienced local guides. You just sit back, take photos, and enjoy the scenery while we handle the driving.

Can I drive my own motorbike (self-drive)?

Yes. If you are an experienced rider with the correct license, you can ride your own bike alongside our guide. We provide the motorcycle and fuel for the same price as the guided option.

What happens if it rains?

Mountain weather is unpredictable. We provide high-quality rain jackets, pants, and boot covers for all riders. If the weather becomes dangerous, we pause the tour or adjust the route. Safety dictates the schedule.

Are meals included in the tour price?

Breakfast is usually included at our overnight homestays. Lunch and dinner are not included in the base price. This gives you the flexibility to choose what you want to eat. Expect to spend about 150.000–300.000 ₫ per day on food (≈ €5–10).

Is it safe for solo female travelers?

Absolutely. We host solo female travelers every week. Our guides are highly professional, respectful, and protective of our guests. Vietnam is generally a very safe country for tourists.

Where do we stay overnight on multi-day tours?

We avoid sterile tourist hotels. We sleep in local guesthouses, private rooms, and traditional ethnic minority homestays (like the M'Nong longhouses near Lak Lake). Accommodation is clean, safe, and authentic.

How many hours a day do we actually ride?

Depending on the route, we spend between 4 to 6 hours actively riding per day. We break the journey up with constant stops for sightseeing, coffee, meals, and photos.

Can the itinerary be customized?

Yes. Because we keep our groups small (maximum 10 people), we can easily adjust the route. If you want to spend more time at a waterfall or skip a pagoda, just tell your guide.

What is the best time of year for a Vietnam motorbike tour?

The Central Highlands are great year-round, but the dry season from November to April offers the best riding conditions. The rainy season (May to October) is greener and waterfalls are more spectacular, but you must be prepared for afternoon showers.

Are there frequent stops for bathroom breaks?

Yes. We stop frequently at local cafes, gas stations, and rest areas. If you need to stop at any point, just tap your guide on the shoulder.

I am vegetarian or vegan. Will I find food in the mountains?

Yes. Buddhism is prominent in Vietnam, so vegetarian food (chay) is widely available even in rural areas. Your guide will ensure your dietary requirements are communicated to the local restaurants.

What happens if a motorbike breaks down?

Our bikes are strictly maintained, but mechanical issues can happen on rural roads. Our guides are trained mechanics and carry toolkits. If a bike cannot be fixed quickly, we will arrange a replacement vehicle immediately so your tour is not delayed.

What protective riding gear do you provide?

We provide high-quality, safety-certified helmets and full-body rain gear for all riders. We recommend you wear long pants, closed-toe shoes, and sunglasses for wind protection.

Can families with children join the tour?

Yes, children can ride safely on the back of our guides' motorbikes. We have smaller helmets and safety belts specifically designed for younger passengers. Please contact us in advance to discuss the age and size of your children.

Do you pick me up from my hotel?

Yes. All tours include free pickup from your hotel or hostel in Dalat. For tours ending in other cities (like Hoi An or Saigon), we drop you off directly at your next accommodation or bus station.

What is your cancellation and refund policy?

We require a 20% deposit to secure your booking. If you cancel more than 48 hours before the tour, your deposit is fully refunded. If you cancel within 48 hours, the deposit is non-refundable. We offer a 100% money-back guarantee if the tour quality falls short of our promises.

How do I pay the remaining balance?

The 20% deposit is paid online during booking. The remaining 80% is paid directly to your guide on the first day of the tour. We accept cash (Vietnamese Dong, EUR, or USD) or credit cards via our secure mobile terminal.

Should I tip my Easy Rider guide?

Tipping is not mandatory in Vietnam, but it is highly appreciated. If your guide went above and beyond to keep you safe and show you an amazing time, a tip of 150.000–300.000 ₫ per day (≈ €5–10) is a standard gesture of gratitude.
